    Blue Plaques in Leeds

    Blue Plaques are signs erected in public places, that commemorate historic events associated with that location, and the lives of historic people who have had an association with the location. The earliest and most widely established scheme iis run in London, an is now administered by English Heritage; separately administered schemes are run in many other parts of the country.

     

    Leeds Civic Trust set up its Blue Plaques Scheme in 1987 to promote public awareness of the heritage of the city of Leeds. The first chosen blue plaque - Burley Bar, was unveiled in 1987. Right now, there are over 110 historical hertiages received this honour.
